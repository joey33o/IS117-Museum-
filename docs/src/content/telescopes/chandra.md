---
title: "Chandra X-ray Observatory"
description: "Launched by Space Shuttle Columbia in 1999, Chandra is NASA's flagship X-ray telescope and part of the Great Observatories fleet — eight times sharper and able to detect sources twenty times fainter than any previous X-ray mission."
image: "/images/chandra.jpg"
---

Because X-ray photons penetrate conventional mirrors rather than reflecting from them, Chandra's four pairs of mirrors are shaped like glass barrels and aligned nearly parallel to incoming X-rays so the photons graze off the surface. Polished at Raytheon Optical Systems to the smoothness of a few atoms, the mirrors were then coated with iridium — a rare, highly reflective metal — at Optical Coating Laboratories in California.

The completed mirrors were assembled into a High Resolution Mirror Assembly at Eastman Kodak in Rochester, New York, aligned end-to-end across 2.7 metres to an accuracy of 1.3 micrometers — roughly one-fiftieth the width of a human hair. Chandra is managed by NASA's Marshall Center and operated from the Chandra X-ray Center (CXC) at the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory, giving scientists worldwide the ability to image the extreme environments where matter is heated to millions of degrees by powerful magnetic fields, intense gravity, and explosive forces.
