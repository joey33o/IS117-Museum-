title: "Hubble Space Telescope"
description: "Launched in 1990 by Space Shuttle Discovery, Hubble orbits 300 miles above Earth and has made more than 1.7 million observations over 35 years, fundamentally changing our understanding of the cosmos."
image: "/images/hubble.jpg"
---

Named in honor of astronomer Edwin Hubble, the telescope's 94.5-inch primary mirror detects light from ultraviolet through near-infrared (115–2500 nanometers), enabling it to peer back 13.4 billion light-years to the early universe. At 43.5 feet long and traveling at 17,000 mph, it completes one orbit every 95 minutes.

Five astronaut servicing missions replaced and upgraded instruments over the telescope's lifetime, keeping it at the cutting edge of science. Its current instruments — the Advanced Camera for Surveys, Cosmic Origins Spectrograph, Space Telescope Imaging Spectrograph, and Wide Field Camera 3 — have contributed to more than 22,000 peer-reviewed scientific papers. Hubble has watched a comet collide with Jupiter, discovered moons around Pluto, and probed the supermassive black holes that lurk at the centers of galaxies.
